Wealth

One of the resources, wealth can be simply collected by making farms, but that is very inefficient. The best way to collect wealth is to build fields on commodities. In a particularly traded commodity, say, poultry, if you own the only poultry field, you gain 50 wealth. The more poultry fields you own, the more wealth you gain, up to 100 wealth. Your gain is also a percentage of your market share. The exact formula is 50 × market share × (2 - 1 ÷ owned fields).

Scarce commodities are therefore likely to be more valuable. Pockets of a single commodity contain a lot of value and are easier to defend. Focusing on a few commodities can allow you to own more of that market, and get more value out of it.

On the flip side, if you want to reduce the wealth of an enemy that relies on a particular commodity, try to own a region of the map where that commodity is very present. You can also attack their fields.

Wealth is necessary to build monuments and stock exchanges, which you need three of for a Prosperity Victory, two of for an Industrial Victory, one of for an Intellectual Victory.
